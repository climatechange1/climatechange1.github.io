Introduction

Climate change is currently a major poltical. If no action is taken to reduce global warming, it will cause major lasting effects that can not be reversed. It effects include "extreme weather events,declining air quality, and destablizing natural systems due to increase in greenhouse gas emissions"(Climate Change and Human Health).
As you progress through this website I will present how climate change will effect you and how you can take action to prevent climate change from getting worse. So, you can enjoy this earth and its resources as my generation and generations before have enjoyed this earth.

Warming Oceans

Oceans are major carbon sink meaing they absorb alot of carbon dixoide overtime. However, with more carbon dixoide being released then ocean can not handle means ocean are getting exteremly hot. Which results in ocean absorbing less carbon dixoide from the atmosphere. Also, Hotter ocean leads to fish moving toward the north pole where the cold ocean is, which leads to fishing industries being severely hurt and also hurting communities that live near the ocean and rely on it for there as there main consumption and livelihood.

Melting Ice Sheets

Another major effect of is melting ice sheets which causes sea levels to rise, meaning flooding will increase due to it and also cities near the ocean and beaches can potentially come under water. "Approximately Greenland lost an average of 286 billion tons of ice per year between 1993 and 2016"(NASA). "The rate of Antarctica ice mass loss has tripled in the last decade"(NASA). This leads to more warming, earth overall will be much hotter and in the tropics some places can become inhabitat, leading emigration from these lands.

Extreme Events

Change is climate change has another danger which is increase in extreme events. Due to rising sea levels and hotter weather there will be increase in extreme natural events such Hurricane, Tornado, Heat Waves, Increasing amount of Rainfalls, and Wildfire. Most of these cause major disruption to daily lives to those affected by it. Increases in wildfires is already prominent in areas such as Australia and California; these areas were heavily affected last year and will most likely be affected this year aswell.

How you can help

While you might not be able make a change on big scale. You can definitely make changes to your lifestyle that will decrease your carbon footprint and slow down climate change. You can reduce your carbon footprint by using the three R's: reduce, reuse, and recycle.

Reduce your use of plastic products such as spoons, forks, and plates. When doing a takeout ask resturant to include only things you need such as if you do not want forks and spoons if you already have them at home.

Reuse is one of the simplest things to include in your lifestyle. For example, instead using plastic water bottles and then throwing them away just use a water bottle container. Instead of using a paper bag and plastic ziplock your lunch instead use plastic container with lids. While some of these cost more upfront however in the long run they help you save more, and simultaneously reduce your carbon footprint.

Recyle is the easiest method to reduce your carbon footprint and that is to just recyle your waterbottles, metals cans, paper, etc.
